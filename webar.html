WebAR: augmented reality that runs at a URL

No app store, no install, no account. A visitor scans a QR code or taps a link and the camera, tracking, and 3D all run inside the phone browser. This is the category every project below was actually shipped in, so it gets the most worked examples.

Where this shows up in shipped work

Uncanny Garden

Three.js plus MediaPipe HandLandmarker: five elements bloom from hand gestures, tracked entirely in-browser with no marker and no server. Shows the ceiling of what raw Three.js plus a tracking library can do without any hosted AR SDK.

Elemental WebAR (RMIT teaching kit)

A-Frame plus MindAR image tracking, config-driven: one JSON entry and two asset files per student work, no code changes needed to add a new piece. Shows the gentler on-ramp for students who have never touched 3D code.

Touch of Water

Three.js with DRACOLoader-compressed models and Howler.js audio, paired with a Leaflet satellite map landing page. Five student teams each contributed a GPS point, model, and sound to one shared engine.

gelora-soil (vanitas)

Three.js plus MediaPipe HandLandmarker and FaceLandmarker together: a procedural flower rooted to a palm or grown from a mouth, with self-hosted MediaPipe models and wasm to avoid depending on a CDN at a venue with restricted wifi.

A recurring lesson across these projects: MediaPipe's wasm runtime and model files are CDN-hosted by default. Venue wifi that cannot reach jsdelivr or Google's storage will break hand or face tracking silently. Self-hosting those files under public/ is the fix, and gelora-soil already does this.
