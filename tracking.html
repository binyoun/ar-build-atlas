Tracking approaches

This is the layer underneath every platform in the other four categories: the actual technique used to anchor virtual content to the real world. Picking the tracking approach usually narrows the platform choice faster than starting from the platform.

Option What it is Cost Learning curve Distribution Offline capable Vibecoding fit
Image / marker tracking Recognizes a flat printed image (MindAR, Vuforia, 8th Wall image targets) and anchors content to it. The most reliable and lowest-compute option, at the cost of needing a physical printed marker. Free (MindAR) to subscription (Vuforia, 8th Wall) Low to medium Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High, with MindAR specifically.
Markerless SLAM / plane detection The phone maps the room live and tracks its own position in it, no printed marker needed (ARKit, ARCore, WebXR hit-test). Free, bundled into the platform Medium to high: more compute, more edge cases (lighting, texture-poor surfaces) Depends on the chosen platform Yes Medium, and platform-dependent.
Face tracking A live face mesh drives effects (MediaPipe FaceLandmarker, ARKit ARFaceAnchor, or Lens Studio and Spark's built-in face effects). Free (MediaPipe, ARKit, ARCore) to platform-locked (Lens Studio, Spark) Medium Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High, with MediaPipe specifically.
Hand tracking Live hand landmarks drive gesture and interaction (MediaPipe HandLandmarker, or ARKit/Vision hand pose). Free Medium Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High, with MediaPipe specifically.
Location-based / GPS AR Anchors content to real-world coordinates rather than a visual feature, so it appears at the same physical spot for anyone standing there. Free (raw geolocation) to subscription (8th Wall geo tools) Medium: GPS accuracy on phones is loose, usually a few meters Depends on the chosen platform Partial, needs a live GPS fix Medium. Straightforward code, but accuracy is a hardware limit, not a coding one.
Cloud anchors A 3D anchor saved to a server so multiple visitors see the same virtual object fixed to the same real spot, together or at different times (ARCore Cloud Anchors, Azure Spatial Anchors). Free tier then usage-based Medium to high: adds a backend and multi-device sync on top of tracking itself Depends on the chosen platform No, needs the anchor-resolving service Medium. The client code is agent-friendly; the shared-state backend is a genuinely new piece, not just more of the same.

Which of Bin's projects use which

Image / marker tracking

Elemental WebAR (RMIT teaching kit) uses MindAR image targets, one printed or photographed marker per student work.

Hand tracking

Uncanny Garden's five-element gesture system and gelora-soil's default palm-rooted growth mode both use MediaPipe HandLandmarker.

Face tracking

gelora-soil's "grow from the mouth" mode uses MediaPipe FaceLandmarker, deriving openness, stability, and tilt from face mesh geometry instead of a hand.

An honest gap. Touch of Water uses GPS, but only to place editions on a Leaflet landing map, not to anchor the AR content itself. No project here yet uses markerless SLAM, true location-anchored AR, or cloud anchors. A multi-visitor, shared-anchor piece would be the first real reason to reach for that last one.
