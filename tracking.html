Tracking approaches

This is the layer underneath every platform in the other four categories: the actual technique used to anchor virtual content to the real world. Picking the tracking approach usually narrows the platform choice faster than starting from the platform.

Option What it is Cost Learning curve Distribution Offline capable Vibecoding fit Used in
Image / marker tracking Recognizes a flat printed image (MindAR, Vuforia, 8th Wall image targets) and anchors content to it. The most reliable and lowest-compute option, at the cost of needing a physical printed marker. Free (MindAR) to subscription (Vuforia, 8th Wall) Low to medium Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High, with MindAR specifically. Elemental WebAR (RMIT teaching kit)
Object tracking Recognizes and tracks a specific 3D object, not just a flat image, so content anchors to it correctly from any angle (Vuforia Object Targets, ARKit Object Detection against a pre-scanned reference object). Free (ARKit) to subscription (Vuforia) Medium to high; needs a pre-scanned 3D reference of the object, not just a photo Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted Medium. The scanning and reference-object step is a real workflow step outside code, not something an agent can shortcut. Not yet used
Markerless SLAM / plane detection The phone maps the room live and tracks its own position in it, no printed marker needed (ARKit, ARCore, WebXR hit-test). Free, bundled into the platform Medium to high: more compute, more edge cases (lighting, texture-poor surfaces) Depends on the chosen platform Yes Medium, and platform-dependent. Not yet used
Face tracking A live face mesh drives effects (MediaPipe FaceLandmarker, ARKit ARFaceAnchor, or Lens Studio and Spark's built-in face effects). Free (MediaPipe, ARKit, ARCore) to platform-locked (Lens Studio, Spark) Medium Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High, with MediaPipe specifically. gelora-soil (vanitas), grow-from-the-mouth mode
Hand tracking Live hand landmarks drive gesture and interaction (MediaPipe HandLandmarker, or ARKit/Vision hand pose). Free Medium Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High, with MediaPipe specifically. Uncanny Garden, gelora-soil (vanitas), palm mode
Body / pose tracking Full-body skeletal tracking (MediaPipe Pose Landmarker, ARKit and ARCore's body tracking) drives effects from a person's whole posture rather than a hand or face alone. Free (MediaPipe, ARKit, ARCore) Medium, similar profile to hand and face tracking Depends on the chosen platform Yes, if self-hosted High with MediaPipe specifically, same as hand and face tracking above. Not yet used
Location-based / GPS AR Anchors content to real-world coordinates rather than a visual feature, so it appears at the same physical spot for anyone standing there. Free (raw geolocation) to subscription (8th Wall geo tools) Medium: GPS accuracy on phones is loose, usually a few meters Depends on the chosen platform Partial, needs a live GPS fix Medium. Straightforward code, but accuracy is a hardware limit, not a coding one. Touch of Water, landing map placement only, not AR anchoring itself
Depth / LiDAR sensing A dedicated depth sensor (LiDAR on Pro-model iPhones and iPads) gives precise real-world geometry: accurate occlusion of virtual objects behind real ones, instant plane detection, and room-scale meshing without camera-only SLAM's guesswork. Free API, but requires LiDAR-equipped hardware specifically Medium, mostly ARKit's depth and scene-geometry APIs iOS only, and only on LiDAR-equipped devices Yes Medium, same constraint as native ARKit work generally. Not yet used
Cloud anchors A 3D anchor saved to a server so multiple visitors see the same virtual object fixed to the same real spot, together or at different times (ARCore Cloud Anchors, Azure Spatial Anchors). Free tier then usage-based Medium to high: adds a backend and multi-device sync on top of tracking itself Depends on the chosen platform No, needs the anchor-resolving service Medium. The client code is agent-friendly; the shared-state backend is a genuinely new piece, not just more of the same. Not yet used
The pattern worth noticing: everything with a "used" entry above is a technique MediaPipe or MindAR handles for free, in-browser. Everything still marked "not yet used" either needs specialized hardware (LiDAR), a pre-scanned physical reference (object tracking), or a real backend (cloud anchors). None of that is out of reach, it is just a different, larger kind of project than a single WebAR page.
