No-code and creator platforms

Visual, node-based editors built by social platforms for fast filters and effects, plus a smaller set of platforms built specifically for artists attaching AR to physical work. The trade you make here is speed and reach for a specific platform or gallery use case, against ownership of the pipeline and, in most cases, the code itself.

Artist and gallery platforms

A smaller, less code-oriented category worth knowing about specifically because it targets the use case a lens-based and installation artist actually has: attaching AR to a printed photograph, canvas, or gallery wall label, without building any tracking yourself.

Artivive A platform built specifically for artists: upload a video or animation, tag a printed image, canvas, or artwork as the trigger, and visitors view it through the Artivive app or an embeddable web view. Free tier for a small number of works, paid tiers for more Very low. Upload media, tag a trigger image, done. EyeJack Another artist and gallery-focused AR platform, similar image-trigger model to Artivive, with its own app and curation tools aimed at exhibitions and print/canvas work. Subscription, tiered Low EyeJack app No Low, same profile as Artivive above. Not yet used
Worth a real look for gallery contexts. Artivive and EyeJack solve a narrower problem than everything else on this page: an audience member looking at a physical print or canvas, with a phone, and no QR code friction the platform hasn't already solved. For a piece that lives on a wall rather than in someone's hand from the start, one of these two may beat building a custom WebAR image-tracking flow from scratch, purely on visitor-experience grounds, even though neither is a coding surface an agent can help much with.
Ownership status changes fast in this category overall. Meta has scaled back public access to Spark AR/Meta Spark before, and platform-owned tools like this can be paused or closed to new creators with little notice. Confirm current public availability before planning real work around any row in either table above.
